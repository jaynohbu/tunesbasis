Music Data

The fundamental signal that constitutes music is the tone. It is a form of sensation, and can be analyzed in terms of pitch, loudness, duration, and timbre.

Pitch refers to the perceived height of sound, determined by the frequency of vibration â€” the greater the number of vibrations per second, the higher the pitch.

Loudness, or intensity, measures the amplitude of vibration, representing the strength or magnitude of the sound wave.

The temporal structure of a musical signal consists of its onset (beginning), offset (end), and tempo, which together define the duration of sounds and form the basis of rhythm.

Finally, timbre, often described as the color or quality of sound, distinguishes one instrument or voice from another even when pitch and loudness are identical.
